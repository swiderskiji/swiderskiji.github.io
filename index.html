Hi. I’m J. Swiderski. I’m a transportation advocate turned transportation planner, a Hokie motivated by the spirit of Ut Prosim — “That I may serve” — long before I began my graduate studies at Virginia Tech’s Planning program, and a Columbia Heights resident running for Advisory Neighborhood Commissioner for SMD 1B03.

What’s my story?

While working for a non-profit affiliated with the University of Pittsburgh in the early 2010s, I got involved with several neighborhood livable-streets advocacy organizations Bike Pittsburgh started, and helped found a couple. I was on the board of Better Streets Lawrenceville for a couple years.

Eventually, I decided I didn’t just want to go to meetings and complain about inadequate proposals, I wanted to be a part of making better ones, so in 2017, I quit my job, moved to DC, and started school in transportation planning. Seven years later, I’m now a Planner with Toole Design Group, one of the leading firms for transportation planning and engineering that puts people first, rather than simply seeking to move more cars faster.
